Sync Licensing 101 for Independent Artists

How to get your music placed in TV, film, ads, and games — and earn real money from it.

What Is Sync Licensing?

Sync (synchronization) licensing is when your music is paired with visual media — TV shows, films, commercials, video games, trailers, and online content. It's one of the most lucrative revenue streams for independent artists because it pays upfront fees AND generates backend royalties.

A single sync placement can earn anywhere from $500 to $500,000+ depending on the usage.

Preparing Your Catalog

Clean Rights

  • Own 100% of your master recording — this is the #1 advantage indie artists have
  • Know your publishing splits — if you co-wrote, get splits agreed in writing
  • No uncleared samples — music supervisors won't touch tracks with sample issues
  • Register with a PRO (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC) for backend performance royalties

Metadata Matters

  • Complete ISRC codes for every track
  • Accurate songwriter/publisher credits
  • Genre, mood, tempo, and instrumentation tags
  • Lyrics (clean and explicit versions if applicable)
  • BPM and key information
Critical: Always have instrumental versions of your songs ready. Music supervisors frequently need vocals-free versions for dialogue scenes.

Getting Placed: Three Paths

1. Sync Agents / Licensing Companies

They pitch your music to supervisors on your behalf. They take 25-50% of sync fees but have relationships you don't.

Top sync libraries accepting indie submissions:

  • Musicbed, Artlist, Epidemic Sound (production music)
  • Position Music, Terrorbird, Secret Road (traditional sync)
  • Songtradr, Tunefind (marketplace model)

2. Direct Pitching

Contact music supervisors directly — harder but keeps 100% of fees.

  • Research what shows/brands use indie music
  • Find supervisors on LinkedIn, Guild of Music Supervisors directory
  • Send brief, professional emails with streaming links (not attachments)
  • Follow up once. If no response, move on.

3. Sync Licensing Platforms

Upload your catalog and let supervisors find you:

  • Musicbed — curated, higher-end placements
  • Songtradr — large marketplace, good for volume
  • Marmoset — boutique, artist-friendly

What Music Supervisors Want

  • Authentic emotion — real feeling beats production polish
  • Versatility — songs that fit multiple scenes/moods
  • Quick clearance — they need a yes within hours, not weeks
  • Clean and instrumental versions ready to go
  • Professional metadata so they can find you in searches

Action Steps

  1. Audit your catalog — which tracks have clean rights and sync potential?
  2. Create instrumentals for your top 10 songs
  3. Register all songs with your PRO
  4. Tag metadata (mood, genre, tempo, instrumentation)
  5. Upload to 2-3 sync platforms
  6. Research 5 sync agents and submit your best 5 tracks
