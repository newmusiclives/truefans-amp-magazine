The Ultimate Release Day Checklist

A step-by-step plan for independent artists launching a new single or album, from four weeks out through post-release follow-up.

Releasing music without a plan is like throwing a party and forgetting to send invitations. This checklist breaks the process into four clear phases so nothing slips through the cracks. Print it out, check off each item, and give your music the launch it deserves.

Phase 1 Four Weeks Before Release

The foundation phase. Everything here sets the stage for a strong release day.

  • Finalize master and get your tracks delivered to your distributor (DistroKid, TuneCore, CD Baby, or AWAL) at least 3-4 weeks early.
  • Register your songs with your Performing Rights Organization (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC, or your local PRO).
  • Set up or claim your Spotify for Artists, Apple Music for Artists, and Amazon Music for Artists profiles.
  • Pitch your lead single to Spotify editorial playlists via Spotify for Artists (must be done at least 7 days before release).
  • Create cover art in all required sizes: 3000x3000 px (distribution), 1080x1080 (social), 1920x1080 (YouTube).
  • Write your press release: one-sheet with bio, high-res photos, streaming links, and a quote.
  • Build a pre-save landing page using Linkfire, Feature.fm, or ToneDen.
  • Send advance copies to playlist curators, bloggers, and local press. Aim for 20-30 contacts minimum.
  • Plan and batch-create 2-3 weeks of social media content: teasers, countdowns, behind-the-scenes clips.
  • Record a short video (60 seconds) explaining the story behind the song for Instagram Reels and TikTok.
Pro Tip: Create a shared Google Sheet with every contact you pitch. Track who replied, who posted, and follow up politely after 5-7 days.

Phase 2 Release Week (7 Days Out)

Build momentum and confirm every moving piece is in place.

  • Confirm your release is showing as scheduled in your distributor dashboard.
  • Post your first teaser: a 15-second audio clip or lyric snippet on Instagram Stories, TikTok, and X.
  • Send a "coming this Friday" email to your mailing list with the pre-save link.
  • Update your Spotify for Artists profile: pick an Artist Pick, update your bio, and set your new release as the header.
  • Schedule release-day posts across all platforms (Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, X, YouTube Community).
  • Prepare your YouTube content: lyric video, visualizer, or official music video set to premiere on release day.
  • Reach out to 5-10 friends, collaborators, or fellow artists and ask them to share on release day.
  • Set up a release-day Instagram Live or TikTok Live listening session.
  • Test all links: pre-save page, website, social bios, Linktree or equivalent.

Phase 3 Release Day

Today is the day. Focus on visibility and engagement in the first 24 hours.

  • Post your main announcement on every platform as soon as the release is live. Include direct streaming links.
  • Update your Instagram and TikTok bio links to point to your new release smart link.
  • Share to your Instagram Stories at least 3 times throughout the day (morning, afternoon, evening).
  • Go live on Instagram or TikTok for a listening party. Play the songs, share the stories behind them, answer questions.
  • Send your release-day email to your full list. Include streaming links, a personal note, and a call to action (add to a playlist, share with a friend).
  • Post in relevant Facebook Groups, Reddit communities (r/WeAreTheMusicMakers, r/IndieMusicFeedback), and Discord servers.
  • Text or DM your closest supporters individually. Personal messages drive more saves than broadcast posts.
  • Submit to independent playlist curators on PlaylistPush, SubmitHub, or Dailyplaylists.
  • Thank everyone who shares, posts, or comments publicly. Engagement fuels the algorithm.
Pro Tip: The first 24 hours of streaming data heavily influence Spotify's algorithmic playlists like Release Radar and Discover Weekly. Concentrate your promotional energy here.

Phase 4 Post-Release (Weeks 1-4)

The release is just the beginning. Keep the momentum alive for at least a month.

  • Review your Spotify for Artists and Apple Music analytics. Note which playlists picked you up and where listeners are located.
  • Follow up with any press or curators who haven't responded. Send a polite nudge with updated streaming numbers.
  • Create a second wave of content: reaction videos, acoustic versions, remix teasers, fan-generated content reposts.
  • Pitch yourself for podcast interviews and local radio using your press release and streaming numbers.
  • Run a small targeted ad ($5-10/day) on Instagram or TikTok pointing to your strongest song.
  • Add your new tracks to your own Spotify playlists and cross-promote with other independent artists.
  • Send a follow-up email to your list with a behind-the-scenes story, early reception highlights, or upcoming show dates.
  • Book a release show or livestream within 2-4 weeks of drop. Give fans a reason to stay engaged.
  • Document what worked and what didn't. Write it down while it's fresh. You'll thank yourself next release.
Remember: A great release is a campaign, not a moment. Artists who sustain promotion for 4-6 weeks consistently outperform those who post once and move on.
