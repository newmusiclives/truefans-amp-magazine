Studio Update

"Let Your Love Flow" — Out Now

Our take on the Bellamy Brothers classic dropped last year and it's still finding new ears every week, so if you somehow missed it — go give it a spin. We didn't want to do a faithful cover. We wanted to take the song apart and rebuild it with the band that we are right now: Ashley's voice up front, Dave's guitar grooving underneath, and the rest of the boys giving it that Cosmic Cowgirl push that's become our thing.

If you've heard us play it live, you know what we mean. The studio version has the same energy — we tracked most of it as a full band in one room, the way records used to be made.

Behind The Record

The Blackbird Sessions, Continued

If you've been with us a while, you already know The Blackbird Sessions — the record we tracked at Blackbird Studio in Nashville with some of the best engineers in town. We're still mining that session for new ideas. Two of the songs that almost made the final cut have been creeping back into our live sets, and we're starting to think they deserve a proper second life.

"Highway Light" — A slow-burn closer that didn't fit the pacing of the album. We've been opening with it on the recent tour and people keep coming up after the set asking what it's called. That's usually a sign.

"Only When It's Raining" — This one was almost too jam-band for the record — six-and-a-half minutes, three different feels, a long instrumental section that lets Slice and Scott trade keys. It's the kind of song that only really comes alive live, but we think we know how to capture it now.

Both might end up on a follow-up EP later this year. Newsletter readers will hear them first.

Tour Diary

Three Nights, Three Cities

Asheville, NC — The Grey Eagle
The Grey Eagle is one of those rooms where you can feel the listening before the first note. Sold-out, attentive crowd, and somebody in the third row was singing along to a song we haven't released yet — somebody had been recording us at a previous show. Honored, amused, slightly worried about our setlist surprises.

Bristol, TN — The Birthplace of Country Music
Playing in Bristol is something else. The historic district there is sacred ground for anyone who cares about American music. We did a stripped-down acoustic set in the museum after-hours, just Ashley, Dave, and a guitar. It felt like the right way to honor the room.

Nashville — 3rd and Lindsley
Coming home is always emotional. 3rd & Lindsley has been one of our favorite Nashville rooms since we started, and this run was no exception. The whole band on a great-sounding stage, hometown faces in the crowd, our families up front. We debuted "Highway Light" and the response is what convinced us to put it on the next record.

What We're Listening To

Ashley & Dave's Picks This Month

Ashley's pick: Allison Russell — "The Returner". "Allison's voice carries weight in a way that I think about every time I'm in a vocal booth. She doesn't oversing — she just tells you what's true."

Dave's pick: The Wood Brothers — Heart Is the Hero. "These guys are who I want to be when I grow up. The interplay between Chris and Oliver, the way they leave space for each other — it's a masterclass."

Together: Buddy Guy — The Blues Don't Lie. "We've shared the stage with Buddy and watched him work a room at 87 years old like he had something to prove. This record sounds like a man who has nothing left to prove and is doing it anyway, for love. That's the goal."

A Word About Russ

Always In The Mix

Anyone who saw Sugar Lime Blue before 2020 knew Russ Dean. Our founding bassist, the heart of the low end, the guy who held everything together with a smile that could disarm a bouncer. We lost him in 2020 and we still feel him in every show.

Joe Bass stepping in was never about replacing Russ — nobody can do that. It was about carrying the bass parts forward in a way Russ would respect. Joe came in with that mindset from day one and we couldn't be more grateful.

Every set we play, there's a moment in the middle of "Highway Light" where Dave looks over at the bass amp and just nods. That's for Russ. Always.
