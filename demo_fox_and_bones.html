Tour Diary

Three Shows, Three Stories

Boise, Idaho — The Record Exchange
We played an in-store set at The Record Exchange to about 40 people crammed between the vinyl racks. Halfway through "California," the power went out. We finished the song acoustic, no mics, and the whole room sang the chorus. Sometimes the unplanned moments are the ones you remember forever.

Missoula, Montana — The Top Hat
Missoula always shows up. The crowd was full of familiar faces from past tours — people who've been following us since the early days. One couple told us they got engaged at our show in 2023. We played "Better With You" for them and honestly, we almost cried.

Portland — Mississippi Studios
Coming home is always emotional. Our families were there, friends we haven't seen in months, and a room full of Portland fans who've watched us grow from open mics to headlining. We debuted two new songs and the reaction gave us chills.

Studio Update

New Album: "Wildwood" — Coming Fall 2026

We've been keeping this under wraps, but it's time to share: our third album is called Wildwood, and it's the most personal thing we've ever made.

The album was recorded at Flora Recording Studio here in Portland with producer Tucker Martine (who's worked with The Decemberists, My Morning Jacket, and Neko Case). 11 tracks, all written during a three-week retreat in the Oregon Coast mountains.

What it sounds like: Bigger than our previous records. More instrumentation — pedal steel, strings, even a brass section on one track. But at its core, it's still two voices and a guitar in a room, telling stories about love, loss, and finding your way home.

First single: "Timber Line" drops May 15. Newsletter subscribers get early access — you'll hear it here first, 48 hours before it hits streaming platforms.

What We're Listening To

Sarah & Scott's Picks This Week

Sarah's pick: Adrianne Lenker — "Bright Future". "Every time I listen to this album, I hear something new. The way she layers vulnerability with these almost casual guitar lines — it's like she's having a conversation with you in a room where nobody else exists."

Scott's pick: S. Carey — "Break Me Open". "Sean Carey's solo work is criminally underrated. This track has the most beautiful drum production I've heard all year. It sounds like rain falling on a tin roof."

Together: The War on Drugs — "Live Drugs Again". "We listen to this on every long drive. It's the perfect road music — big, warm, and endlessly comforting."
