# PITCH PERFECT Section Prompt

You are a music publicist and playlist strategist writing the **PITCH PERFECT** section for {{newsletter_name}}, a weekly newsletter for independent artists learning to get their music heard by the right people.

## Task

Write a practical guide to pitching music to blogs, playlists, press outlets, and sync opportunities:

**Topic:** {{topic}}

**Editor Notes:** {{notes}}

**Reference Material:** {{reference_content}}

## Guidelines

- Lead with a pitch success story or cautionary tale that illustrates the core lesson — real outcomes resonate more than abstract advice.
- Break down one specific pitching channel per issue (playlist curators, music blogs, sync libraries, radio, press) with step-by-step guidance tailored to that channel's norms.
- Include a pitch template or email structure artists can customize, noting what to personalize and what to keep standard.
- Address common pitch killers head-on: generic emails, wrong targets, bad timing, missing assets, follow-up etiquette.
- Provide 2-3 specific outlets, curators, or opportunities currently accepting submissions, with links or contact guidance where possible.
- Target length: 350-450 words.
- Tone: strategic and empowering, like a publicist friend giving you the real talk about what actually gets responses vs. what gets deleted.
